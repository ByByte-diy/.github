# ByByte.DIY Code of Conduct

## Our Community

ByByte.DIY™ is an open robotics and STEM education ecosystem built around:

* collaboration;
* engineering education;
* accessibility;
* experimentation;
* and shared learning.

We want the community to be welcoming for:

* beginners;
* students;
* educators;
* makers;
* engineers;
* contributors;
* and people from different backgrounds and experience levels.

The goal of this Code of Conduct is to help maintain a respectful, constructive, and educational environment across the ecosystem.

---

# Expected Behavior

Community members are expected to:

* communicate respectfully;
* help beginners learn;
* provide constructive feedback;
* encourage experimentation and education;
* collaborate professionally;
* respect different experience levels;
* focus on technical and educational discussions;
* contribute positively to the ecosystem.

We encourage:

* patience;
* mentorship;
* curiosity;
* engineering thinking;
* and collaborative problem solving.

---

# Educational Environment

ByByte.DIY is strongly connected to STEM education and may involve:

* children;
* students;
* schools;
* workshops;
* public educational spaces.

Community interactions should remain appropriate for educational environments.

Contributors and community members are expected to behave responsibly and professionally in both public and private project spaces.

---

# Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable within the community:

* harassment or discrimination;
* personal attacks;
* intimidation;
* hate speech;
* trolling;
* intentionally disruptive behavior;
* aggressive or hostile communication;
* deliberate misinformation;
* spam or malicious activity;
* publishing unsafe or intentionally dangerous instructions;
* behavior that creates an unwelcoming environment.

This applies to:

* GitHub repositories;
* discussions;
* issue trackers;
* chat platforms;
* events;
* workshops;
* and community spaces associated with the project.

---

# Technical and Safety Responsibility

Because the ecosystem includes electronics, robotics, and hardware experimentation, contributors should prioritize:

* safety;
* responsible engineering practices;
* clear warnings where needed;
* reproducible instructions;
* educational clarity.

Unsafe, intentionally harmful, or misleading technical content may be removed, and authors who repeatedly publish such content may be restricted or blocked from participating in the community.

---

# Respect for Contributors

Open-source collaboration depends on mutual respect.

Please remember:

* people contribute voluntarily;
* contributors have different skill levels;
* mistakes are part of learning;
* constructive feedback is more valuable than hostility.

Disagreements should remain professional and focused on ideas, implementations, and technical decisions — not individuals.

---

# Project Maintainers

Project maintainers are responsible for:

* guiding the ecosystem;
* reviewing contributions;
* enforcing community standards;
* helping maintain a productive environment.

Maintainers may:

* request changes;
* close discussions;
* reject contributions;
* remove inappropriate content;
* or limit participation if necessary to protect the community and project goals.

---

# Reporting Issues

If you experience or observe unacceptable behavior, please contact the project maintainers.

When reporting issues, please provide:

* context;
* links or screenshots where possible;
* and a clear description of the problem.

Reports should be handled respectfully and responsibly.

---

# Open Collaboration Philosophy

ByByte.DIY is designed to encourage:

* learning;
* experimentation;
* engineering creativity;
* open collaboration;
* and community-driven innovation.

We believe that respectful collaboration creates better technology, better educational materials, and a stronger ecosystem for everyone.

---

# Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

* repositories;
* pull requests;
* issue discussions;
* documentation;
* communication channels;
* official events;
* and community spaces related to ByByte.DIY.

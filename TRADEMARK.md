## Overview

ByByte.DIY™, ByByte Nano™, ByByte Mega™, ByByte NanoBoy™ and associated logos are trademarks of the ByByte.DIY project maintainers.

The ByByte.DIY project is an open robotics and STEM education ecosystem focused on accessible learning, open hardware, open software, and community collaboration.

This policy describes how the ByByte trademarks and branding may be used by the community, educators, contributors, businesses, and third parties.

---

# Open Source and Trademark

The ByByte project uses open-source and open-hardware licenses for software, hardware, and educational materials.

These licenses allow you to:

* use;
* study;
* modify;
* distribute;
* manufacture;
* teach with;
* and commercially use the project materials according to their respective licenses.

However, open-source licensing does not grant rights to use the ByByte trademarks in ways that may imply official affiliation, endorsement, or certification.

---

# Allowed Uses

You may use the ByByte name in the following ways without additional permission.

## Educational Use

You may:

* teach ByByte-based courses;
* create tutorials;
* create educational videos;
* organize workshops;
* use ByByte hardware in schools and clubs.

Examples:

* "Robotics course based on ByByte"
* "Learning Arduino and ByByte robotics"

---

## Community and Open-Source Projects

You may:

* create forks;
* create compatible modules;
* create libraries;
* publish modifications;
* create community projects.

Examples:

* "Compatible with ByByte Nano"
* "ByByte Mega extension board"
* "Community firmware for ByByte robots"

---

## Reselling Original Hardware

You may resell original ByByte hardware or kits if the products are clearly identified as original and unmodified.

---

## Referencing Compatibility

You may truthfully state compatibility with ByByte products.

Examples:

* "Compatible with ByByte Nano"
* "Works with ByByte Mega"

---

# Prohibited Uses

You may NOT:

* claim official partnership without permission;
* imply endorsement by the ByByte project;
* use the ByByte logo as your own brand identity;
* sell modified products as official ByByte products;
* register domains, company names, or social media accounts that impersonate the official project;
* create confusion about the origin of products or services.

---

# Forks and Modified Versions

You may create forks and modified versions of ByByte projects under their respective open-source licenses.

However:

* modified versions should not be presented as official ByByte products;
* significant modifications should use distinct branding;
* users should clearly understand when a project is unofficial.

Recommended wording:

* "Based on ByByte"
* "Fork of ByByte Nano"
* "Unofficial ByByte-compatible platform"

---

# Logo Usage

The official ByByte [logos](img/logo.png) may be used for:

* educational references;
* news articles;
* tutorials;
* reviews;
* community events.

The logos may NOT be:

* modified;
* used as the primary branding of another project;
* used to imply official certification or partnership.

---

# Commercial Use

Commercial use of ByByte open-source projects is allowed according to the respective project licenses.

This includes:

* manufacturing hardware;
* selling kits;
* paid workshops;
* educational courses;
* customization;
* support services;
* integration into commercial solutions.

However, trademark usage must still follow this policy.

---

# Official Projects and Certification

Only projects, products, services, and organizations explicitly approved by the project maintainers may use terms such as:

* "Official ByByte"
* "Certified ByByte"
* "Authorized ByByte Partner"

---

# License References

Different parts of the ByByte ecosystem may use different licenses.

Typical licensing structure:

| Component                 | License       |
| ------------------------- | ------------- |
| Software and firmware     | MIT License   |
| Hardware and PCB designs  | CERN-OHL-S v2 |
| Documentation and lessons | CC BY-SA 4.0  |

Please refer to the LICENSE files of individual repositories for details.

---

# Reporting Misuse

If you believe that a product, organization, or online resource is misusing the ByByte trademarks, please contact the project maintainers.

The latest version of this document should always be considered authoritative.

# ByByte.DIY Brand Guidelines

## Overview

ByByte.DIY™ is an open robotics and STEM education ecosystem focused on accessible learning, open hardware, open software, and community collaboration.

These guidelines describe how the ByByte brand, names, and visual identity should be used across educational materials, community projects, hardware products, and online resources.

The goal of these guidelines is not to restrict the community, but to:

* maintain clarity;
* protect users from confusion;
* preserve trust in the ecosystem;
* and support healthy open collaboration.

---

# Brand Values

The ByByte ecosystem is built around the following principles:

* Accessibility
* Open knowledge
* STEM education
* Creativity
* Engineering mindset
* Community collaboration
* DIY culture
* Learning by building

---

# Referring to ByByte

## Correct Usage

Examples:

* ByByte Nano robot
* ByByte robotics platform
* Compatible with ByByte Mega
* Based on ByByte

---

## Incorrect Usage

Avoid:

* changing capitalization;
* modifying names;
* creating confusingly similar names.

Examples:

* bybyte
* BYBYTE
* ByBite
* OfficialByByteX

---

# Community Projects

Community projects are encouraged.

You may:

* create forks;
* create compatible modules;
* publish tutorials;
* create custom robots;
* organize workshops and clubs.

Please clearly indicate when a project is unofficial.

Recommended wording:

* Community project for ByByte
* Compatible with ByByte
* Based on ByByte
* Unofficial ByByte extension

---

# Official vs Unofficial

Only projects approved by the maintainers may use:

* Official ByByte
* Certified ByByte
* Authorized ByByte Partner

Community projects should avoid language that may imply official affiliation.

---

# Logo Usage

## Allowed Uses

The ByByte logo may be used for:

* tutorials;
* educational materials;
* reviews;
* community events;
* compatibility references;
* articles and videos.

---

## Not Allowed

The logo may not be:

* modified;
* recolored without permission;
* stretched or distorted;
* used as the main branding of another product;
* used to imply official partnership.

---

# Educational Use

Schools, clubs, makerspaces, and educators are encouraged to use ByByte in educational activities.

Allowed:

* classroom use;
* workshops;
* online lessons;
* printed educational materials;
* student projects.

---

# Commercial Use

Commercial activity around the ByByte ecosystem is allowed according to the applicable project licenses.

Examples:

* selling kits;
* educational courses;
* robotics clubs;
* integration services;
* manufacturing compatible hardware;
* customization and support.

Commercial users should avoid presenting modified products as official ByByte.DIY products unless explicitly authorized.

---

# Domain Names and Social Media

Please avoid:

* impersonating official ByByte resources;
* registering misleading domains;
* creating confusing social media accounts.

Examples of problematic names:

* official-bybyte.example
* bybyte-support.example
* bybyte-official-community

---

# Visual Style Recommendations

The ByByte brand should feel:

* technical;
* modern;
* educational;
* accessible;
* maker-oriented;
* engineering-focused.

Recommended visual direction:

* clean layouts;
* readable typography;
* technical illustrations;
* robotics and electronics themes;
* minimal visual clutter.

---

# Open Ecosystem Philosophy

ByByte is designed as an open ecosystem.

We encourage:

* experimentation;
* learning;
* hardware modifications;
* forks;
* educational adaptations;
* community-driven innovation.

The goal is to grow a healthy ecosystem where knowledge and engineering skills are accessible to everyone.
